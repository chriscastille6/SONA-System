You are invited to participate in a research study aimed at understanding decision-making in work settings. The study is being conducted by Dr. Annika Hillebrandt (an Assistant Professor of Industrial/Organizational Psychology at the University of Waterloo, Canada), Dr. Laurie Barclay (a Professor of Management at the University of Guelph, Canada), Caitlin Belfiore (a PhD student at the University of Waterloo, Canada), Megan Herrewynen (a PhD student at the University of Waterloo, Canada), Christine Hwang (a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Guelph, Canada), and Jenny Wang (a PhD student at the University of Guelph, Canada). The study is funded by a grant from the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada. This study is part of a larger, multi-site research project.

Information
You are invited to participate because you hold a University of Waterloo SONA Research account. If you agree to participate in this study, you will work on a decision-making task involving word problems. The purpose of this task is to help us understand how people approach word problems. You will also be asked to complete some measures about the task as well as a basic personality trait measure and demographics. Participation in this study will take no more than 1 hour to complete. 

Risks
There are no known or anticipated risks associated with participation in this study.

Benefits
The researchers cannot guarantee any direct benefits from participation. However, an important contribution of scientific research into organizational behavior is to provide organizations, managers, and employees with data so they can better understand how business and social practices influence employee behavior. In this study, you will be helping us understand the factors that determine employees’ decision making and how managers can influence employees’ behavior.

Confidentiality of Your Information
Approximately 200 students from the University of Waterloo will be invited to complete this study. All data collected will be anonymous. Please do not put any identifying information (e.g., name) on your materials. Any information that could potentially identify participants will be immediately removed from the data. No information that would potentially identify a participant be used in the analyses and/or results. Furthermore, because the interest of this study is in the average responses of the entire group of participants, you will not be identified individually in any way in any written reports of this research. 

Any raw data will be kept for a period of at least 7 years following publication of the research. The data will be stored in a locked office at the University of Waterloo. Electronic copies of the de-identified data may also be stored on secure internal databases or encrypted external drives. Only the researchers affiliated with the University of Waterloo and University of Guelph who are listed at the top of this form will have access to the raw data.

Anonymized data related to your participation will be shared with our collaborators at other universities. It will also be stored on the Open Science Framework (OSF), which is an online platform for data storage and management. The OSF is a secure and reputable platform widely used for research data storage and sharing, ensuring compliance with data protection and privacy standards. The data will also be submitted to a journal (i.e., the data may be publicly available). The data will be completely anonymized prior to submission and will be presented in aggregate form in publications. This process is integral to the research process as it allows other researchers to verify results and avoid duplicating research. Though the data is posted for the purpose listed, other individuals may access these data by accessing the open access repository. Although the dataset without identifiers may be shared publicly, your identity will always remain confidential. Should you not wish to submit your data in this manner, please do not select this study.

Remuneration
For participating in this study, you will receive $7.00 CAD with an opportunity to receive more cash during the study.

Contact 
This study has been reviewed and received ethics clearance through a University of Waterloo Research Ethics Board (REB #46813). If you have questions for the Board, contact the Office of Research Ethics, toll-free at 1-833-643-2379 (Canada and USA), 1-519-888-4440, or reb@uwaterloo.ca.

For all other questions, after receiving this letter, or if you would like additional information to assist you in reaching a decision about participation, please feel free to ask the student investigator or a faculty supervisor listed at the top of this sheet.

Participation
Your participation in this study is voluntary. You may decide to withdraw from this study at any time by advising the researcher and may do so without loss of your remuneration. If you withdraw from the study before data collection is completed, you will receive your remuneration, but your data will be destroyed. You have the right to skip any question(s)/procedure(s) you choose. 

Once you have completed the study, you can no longer withdraw your data, as the data will not be identifiable.

Feedback and Publication
The results of the study may be submitted to peer-reviewed academic journals for publication consideration and/or be presented at a conference. If you would like a copy of the results, please visit Dr. Hillebrandt’s website (https://uwaterloo.ca/justice-affect-and-morality-lab/) for research results after January 1, 2027.
