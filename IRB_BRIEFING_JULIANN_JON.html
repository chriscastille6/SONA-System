6. Federal Audit Compliance (No Credit/Bonus)

Context: SONA/PRAMS is live but shadow IT. We will not use course credit or monitor bonus points. What is required to survive a federal audit?

Why no credit/bonus changes FERPA: Without credit/bonus, participation is voluntary research—not used for grading. Per Cornell IRB and case law (Owasso v. Falvo, Hardin County Schools v. Foster), voluntary research data not used for institutional decisions is not FERPA-protected education records.

Minimum Requirements

45 CFR 46 (Common Rule):

  • IRB oversight for all studies
  • Informed consent documented
  • Risk minimization; data security (TLS, access controls)

FERPA:

  • Greatly reduced without credit/bonus
  • Keep research data anonymous or de-identified
  • No link between participation and grades

Data Security:

  • TLS/HTTPS
  • Role-based access
  • Audit logging for protocol submissions, IRB decisions, exports

Audit Trail:

  • Who submitted, when, decision
  • Who approved, when
  • Log data exports; log AI API calls (hash only)

Data Retention:

  • Document policy (e.g., 5 years post-study)
  • Purge or anonymize when no longer needed

Disable Without Credit

  • CreditTransaction — disable
  • Bonus-point tracking — disable
  • Student ID for research participants — don't collect

Formalization Options

Recommended Minimum

  1. Disable credit allocation and bonus-point features
  2. Use anonymous participation (session_id, no participant FK)
  3. Document that participation is voluntary and not used for grades
  4. Maintain IRB approval for all studies
  5. Add audit logging for protocol submissions, IRB decisions, exports
  6. Restrict self-registration to participant role only
  7. Retain consent records and protocol documentation per IRB
