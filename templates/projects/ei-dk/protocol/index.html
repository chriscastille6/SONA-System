Study Purpose

This study examines metacognitive accuracy in emotional intelligence‚Äîhow well people can estimate their own EI abilities. Research shows that lower performers often overestimate their abilities (the "Dunning-Kruger effect"), while higher performers may underestimate theirs.

Important Caveats

  • This is correlational research‚Äîwe're looking for patterns across many people, not diagnosing individuals
  • Your individual feedback is based on preliminary norms and should be interpreted cautiously
  • Short tests have measurement error; your "true" ability may differ from today's score
  • These results don't define you‚ÄîEI is developable through practice and training

What Happens Next?

Once we collect data from at least 20 participants, we'll analyze:

  • Correlation between actual performance and self-estimates (calibration)
  • Relationship between ability level and over/underestimation (bias)
  • How feedback affects interest in EI training
