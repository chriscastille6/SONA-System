HR Situational Judgment Test: Evidence-Based HR Decision-Making

Full documentation for IRB review. Study slug: hr-sjt. Suggested reviewers: Jon Murphy (CBA), Juliann Allen.

27 HR management scenarios (cases)

Participants rate the effectiveness of HR tactics for each of the following scenario domains. For each scenario, participants rate each available tactic on a 1–5 scale (Not effective to Highly effective).

  • Job analysis
  • Job design
  • Recruiting
  • Selection
  • Performance appraisal
  • Sexual harassment
  • Compensation
  • Workplace accommodations
  • Remote work
  • Bona fide occupational requirements (BFOQ)
  • … and 17 additional HR management scenarios across these and related domains

Full incident text (all cases): Incidents for the Simulation (Google Doc)

Total: 27 scenarios. The full scenario text and tactics are in the live assessment (bayoupal.nicholls.edu/hr-sjt-assessment). This document summarizes the protocol and scenario domains for IRB review.

Protocol summary

Purpose: Compare student vs. HR professional ratings of HR tactics across 27 scenarios. Measure how students evaluate HR management tactics and how training in evidence-based HR practices affects ratings.

Participants: (1) Undergraduate students in MNGT 425 (HR Analytics) and related courses; (2) HR professionals recruited via professional networks.

Procedures: Informed consent → participant ID (no names stored) → HR SJT (27 scenarios; rate effectiveness of tactics 1–5) → feedback PDF → debriefing.

Duration: Approximately 45–60 minutes.

Review type: Exempt (Category D — educational settings, anonymous surveys).
