27 HR management scenarios (cases)

Participants may rate the effectiveness of HR tactics for each scenario on a 1–5 scale (Not effective to Highly effective). Ratings are optional; participants are not forced to answer every tactic or every situation. Each situation also includes an optional notes box so students can describe anything they would like the instructor to know about how they are thinking through that decision.

IRB check — non-forced responding: Each situation includes a Skip this situation control. Rating inputs have no required-field constraints. Reviewers should open the interactive packet (below) and confirm they can leave ratings blank and skip situations.

Protocol summary

Purpose: Compare student vs. HR professional ratings of HR tactics across 27 scenarios. Measure how students evaluate HR management tactics and how training in evidence-based HR practices affects ratings.

Participants: Adults 18+ interested in management topics — (1) MNGT 425 / MNGT 502 students (in-class assignment; Option B qualitative alt graded only, not analyzed); (2) working employees (professionals/managers/executives welcome). Exploratory; no assumed group differences. No PRAMS IDs. No deception / no formal debriefing. Optional emails only via separate lab page /studies/lab/.

Procedures: Informed consent → participant ID (no names stored) → HR SJT (27 scenarios; optional 1–5 effectiveness ratings; skip allowed) → feedback PDF → debriefing.

Duration: Approximately 45–60 minutes.

Review type: Exempt (Category D — educational settings, anonymous surveys).
