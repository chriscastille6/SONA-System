Informed Consent for Participation in Research

Project Title: HR Situational Judgment Test: Evidence-Based HR Decision-Making

Principal Investigator: Dr. Christopher Castille, Management and Marketing

1. Introduction and Purpose

You are invited to an exploratory study on how people interested in management topics — including business students and working employees (professionals, managers, executives) — rate workplace tactics. We do not assume group differences in advance. Participation is VOLUNTARY. Ages 18+.

2. Procedures

  • Optional role category for exploratory comparisons
  • Online HR SJT (27 scenarios; optional ratings/Skip; optional notes)
  • Optional feedback report

No deception; no formal debriefing. Optional emails for aggregate updates / lab studies are collected only on a separate lab form (not formal survey data).

3–7. Benefits, risks, confidentiality

Minimal risk. Research data use an opaque session identifier (no PRAMS ID). Aggregate findings (no individuals identified) may be shared to support community learning and networking around management topics.

8–9. Withdraw / consent

HSIRB Consent verbiage applies. Contact Dr. Christopher Castille — christopher.castille@nicholls.edu.
