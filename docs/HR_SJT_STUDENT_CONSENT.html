Informed Consent for Participation in Research

Project Title: HR Situational Judgment Test: Evidence-Based HR Decision-Making

Principal Investigator: Dr. Christopher Castille, Management and Marketing

1. Introduction and Purpose

You are enrolled in MNGT 425 and/or MNGT 502. As an in-class assignment, you are asked to participate in an exploratory research study on how people interested in management topics (students and working employees) rate workplace tactics. We do not assume group differences in advance. Research participation is VOLUNTARY. You must be 18+.

2. Course Assignment Options

Option A — Research study: Complete the online HR SJT (27 scenarios; optional ratings/Skip; optional decision notes). Ratings use an opaque session identifier (no PRAMS ID). ~45–60 minutes.

Option B — Alternative (not research): Qualitative description of how you would respond to each prompt, submitted to the instructor for equivalent credit. Used only for grading; not analyzed as study data.

No flyer. No deception. No formal debriefing. Optional emails for aggregate updates are collected only on a separate lab form.

3–7. Procedures, benefits, risks, confidentiality, voluntary credit

Research ratings are not stored with names/emails. Both Option A and Option B satisfy the assignment. Choosing Option B does not reduce your grade relative to Option A.

8–10. Withdraw / deletion / consent

HSIRB Consent verbiage applies. Contact Dr. Christopher Castille — christopher.castille@nicholls.edu — to withdraw or request deletion.
