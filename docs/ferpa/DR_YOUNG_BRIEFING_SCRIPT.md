## 30-second version (read aloud)

> Dr. Young, PRAMS is Nicholls-built software that replaces SONA for **IRB-compliant research recruitment** and hosts **our faculty lab protocols** on **Nicholls servers** — not the cloud. When we run it **without course credit**, student experiment data is stored **anonymously**, separate from academic records. The code goes through **bayouops GitLab and IT review** before anything goes live. We **do not** use banned foreign AI platforms, and we can keep **all production AI turned off** until Nicholls adopts an AI policy. This supports **faculty research and student protection** — it's infrastructure, not an AI product.

**~30 seconds at normal pace**

## 60-second version (if she asks one follow-up)

> The Governor's orders and the Board of Regents are really about **keeping Louisiana data under institutional control** and **not using hostile foreign AI**. PRAMS fits that: it's hosted on **bayoupal**, reviewed through **bayouops**, and doesn't send student GPAs or enrollment data to Google, TikTok, or ChatGPT. The only optional AI piece is an **administrative tool for IRB staff** to review protocol text — and we can run that on **our own server** or **disable it completely**. Faculty development used standard coding tools, but **no real student data** ever goes into that process. We're happy to walk IT through a one-page inventory and turn off anything leadership wants off until policy is in place.

---

## If she asks: "Is this safe for Nicholls?"

> Yes — because it's **reviewed, hosted, and controlled by us**. The risk stories in the news are about **student portals leaking data to advertisers**. PRAMS is the opposite: **self-hosted, minimal third parties, anonymous research storage.**
