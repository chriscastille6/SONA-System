Goals as Reference Points and Risk Taking — Full Vignette Pool

Participants are randomly assigned 3–6 vignettes from this pool. Each vignette is presented in one of two goal frames (below goal / above goal) as determined by the study design.

Response types

  • Replication: Binary choice: (A) sure option vs. (B) risky option
  • Extension: Likert rating: How acceptable was [person]'s decision? 1 = Not at all acceptable … 7 = Completely acceptable

Vignettes

Vignette R1 — ReplicationHeath et al. (1999); Larrick et al. (2009)

Goal frames: below, above

Scenario (below goal):
You have a goal of selling 10 units this month. You have sold 8 units so far (below goal). You have one more opportunity to make a sale.

Scenario (above goal):
You have a goal of selling 10 units this month. You have sold 12 units so far (above goal). You have one more opportunity to make a sale.

Option A: Accept a sure outcome: 1 additional unit sold (total 9 below / 13 above).

Option B: Take a risk: 50% chance of 2 units sold, 50% chance of 0 units (total 8 or 10 below / 12 or 14 above).

Vignette R2 — ReplicationAu & Feldman (2020)

Goal frames: below, above

Scenario (below goal):
You need to reach a fundraising goal of $5,000. You currently have $4,200 (below goal). One donor is willing to give a guaranteed $300.

Scenario (above goal):
You have exceeded your fundraising goal of $5,000 and currently have $5,400. One donor is willing to give a guaranteed $300.

Option A: Accept the guaranteed $300.

Option B: Ask the donor to flip a coin: heads they give $600, tails they give $0.

Vignette E1 — ExtensionNovel (extension)

Goal frames: below, above

Scenario (below goal):
Alex is a salesperson who needed to hit a quarterly goal to keep a bonus. With one day left, Alex was $200 short. Alex submitted an expense report that included $250 in personal meals as "client entertainment."

Scenario (above goal):
Alex is a salesperson who had already exceeded the quarterly goal and secured the bonus. Alex submitted an expense report that included $250 in personal meals as "client entertainment."

Question: How acceptable was Alex's decision to submit this expense report?

Scale: 1 = Not at all acceptable … 7 = Completely acceptable

Vignette E2 — ExtensionNovel (extension)

Goal frames: below, above

Scenario (below goal):
Jordan is a manager who was below the department's safety-incident goal for the year. A minor incident occurred that was not reported to central safety. Jordan did not add it to the monthly report.

Scenario (above goal):
Jordan is a manager whose department had already met the safety-incident goal for the year. A minor incident occurred that was not reported to central safety. Jordan did not add it to the monthly report.

Question: How acceptable was Jordan's decision not to include the incident in the report?

Scale: 1 = Not at all acceptable … 7 = Completely acceptable
