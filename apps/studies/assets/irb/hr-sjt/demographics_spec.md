# HR SJT Demographics Measure

This study uses a demographic measure that combines **BLS/EEO-1 race-ethnicity categories** (standard across PRAMS assessments) with **HR-professional-specific items** based on Rynes et al. (2002) and Fisher et al. (2021).

## BLS / EEO-1 Race and Ethnicity (all participants)

Collected for comparability with federal reporting and other PRAMS studies. Optional.

| Value | Label |
|-------|--------|
| (blank) | Prefer not to answer |
| hispanic-latino | Hispanic or Latino |
| white | White (Not Hispanic or Latino) |
| black-african-american | Black or African American |
| asian | Asian |
| american-indian-alaska-native | American Indian or Alaska Native |
| native-hawaiian-pacific-islander | Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander |
| two-or-more | Two or More Races |

*Source: BLS/EEO-1 categories; same as used in EI-DK and other platform studies.*

## HR professional demographics (Rynes et al., 2002; Fisher et al., 2021)

Shown **only when the participant is an HR professional** (working professional / professional cohort). Used to compare beliefs and tactic ratings by credential, job level, and experience.

### 1. Job level

| Value | Label |
|-------|--------|
| other | Other |
| hr_manager | HR Manager |
| director | Director |
| vp | VP (Vice President) |

*Rynes et al. coded job level 1–4; higher level and SPHR certification were associated with greater agreement with research findings.*

### 2. Years in HR

- **Tenure** (number of years in HR or HR-related role).  
- Can be collected as open number or bands (e.g., &lt;2, 2–5, 6–10, 11+).

### 3. Highest level of education

| Value | Label |
|-------|--------|
| hs | High school |
| bachelors | Bachelor's degree |
| masters | Master's degree |
| phd | Ph.D. or equivalent |

*Rynes et al. coded education 1–4 (H.S., Bachelors, Masters, Ph.D.).*

### 4. HR major (undergraduate or graduate)

- **Yes / No** – Whether the participant’s degree(s) included an HR (or closely related) major.

### 5. Credentials (select all that apply)

| Value | Label |
|-------|--------|
| phr | PHR (Professional in Human Resources) |
| sphr | SPHR (Senior Professional in Human Resources) |
| shrm_cp | SHRM-CP (SHRM Certified Professional) |
| shrm_scp | SHRM-SCP (SHRM Senior Certified Professional) |
| none | None of these |
| other | Other (please specify) |

*Rynes et al. found SPHR certification and job level associated with higher agreement with research; credentials are central for comparing HR professionals’ beliefs and tactic ratings.*

## References

- Rynes, S. L., Colbert, A. E., & Brown, K. G. (2002). HR professionals’ beliefs about effective human resource practices: Correspondence between research and practice. *Human Resource Management*, 41(2), 149–174. (Demographics: job level, tenure, education, HR major, PHR, SPHR.)
- Fisher, P. A., et al. (2021). Selection myths: A conceptual replication of HR professionals’ beliefs about effective human resource practices in the United States and Canada. *Journal of Personnel Psychology*. (Conceptual replication; demographics aligned with Rynes.)
