## 3. Title of Project or Proposal

A Study in Decision Making

## 4. Description

### 4a. Population
VOLUNTARY participation. Adults 18+: (1) Undergraduates in Business Administration courses at Nicholls (esp. BSAD 101, Powell 140); (2) working professionals. Multi-site replication context (Nicholls local site). Under-18 excluded (required for HSIRB exemption). See Attachment A.

### 4b. Procedures
Pilot then main (paper-and-pencil anagram task). Consent → practice rounds → random assignment to do-your-best / mere / personal / reward goal → timed performance rounds → anonymous productivity report; main study adds private self-payment from cash envelope while researcher is out of room → questionnaires → debrief. ~1 hour. Instruments in full HSIRB packet. See Attachment A for full procedure.

### 4c. Objectives
Constructive replication of Schweitzer, Ordóñez & Douma (2004): whether specific performance goals increase overstatement of performance (unethical reporting). Also tests boundary conditions (trait loss aversion; goal proximity) and contributes to a multi-site open-science Registered Report. Hypotheses H1–H5 in Attachment A.

### 5b. Inclusion

### 5c. Exclusion

## 6. Benefits and costs

1. **Subjects:** Pilot: possible course credit/bonus + appreciation letter (no cash). Main: cash via self-payment (≥$7, up to $14, ~$10 typical) + debriefing on open-science replication.
2. **Others:** Managers/employees and scholars: evidence on when goal setting may increase unethical reporting; shared goal-setting / behavioral-ethics understanding.
3. **Society:** Stronger empirical basis for the “dark side” of goal setting; insights to preserve performance benefits while mitigating unethical behavior.

**Payment:** Pilot: no cash. Main: $14 envelope at start; do-your-best/mere/personal keep $10; reward goal keep $7 + $1 per scored round meeting calibrated goal (avg ~$10). Unearned cash returned privately. Course credit/bonus per instructor policy.  
**Time:** Approximately one hour per session (pilot and/or main).  
**Money:** None (participants receive compensation in main study).  
**Repeated testing:** Pilot calibrates materials; separate main session for hypothesis tests. Pilot participants may also do the main study (all data anonymous).
